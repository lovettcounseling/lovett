Hi — I'm Whitney

The short version.

Licensed Professional Counselor (MA, LPC) and Certified Clinical Trauma Professional (CCTP) practicing in Denver. Fifteen-plus years across clinical, educational, and community settings — sitting with adults and couples in some of the hardest moments of their lives, and working alongside teams trying to build systems that actually take care of the people inside them.

My approach is collaborative, relational, trauma-informed, and systems-aware — the kind of therapist who'll sit with you in the hard part and also tell you when it's time to get up. We move at a pace your nervous system can hold. We pay attention to the patterns underneath what's happening. And we work toward something real, not a script.

I work with trauma, anxiety, burnout, chronic stress, life transitions, grief, relationship ruptures, and the slow drift that nobody warns you might happen to your own sense of self. No clipboards. No worksheets that look like the SAT. Just real conversation toward the version of yourself you keep almost reaching.

How this works.

Reach out · Get to it · Keep going

i.

The first call

  • Fifteen minutes, free, no pitch deck and no pressure
  • You tell me what's loud, what's stuck, and what you've already tried
  • I tell you honestly whether I'm the right fit — or who is
ii.

The actual work

  • Weekly or biweekly, in person in Denver or via secure telehealth
  • Pacing that honors your nervous system and your life
  • Goals named in plain language and revisited like they matter
iii.

The keep-going

  • Every 8–12 weeks we check the map against the territory
  • Recalibrate when life rearranges itself — because it always does
  • Wrap when you're ready, with a kit you actually know how to use
